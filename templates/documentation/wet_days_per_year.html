Wet Days Per Year

These endpoints provide access to historical and projected number of wet days per year at a resolution of approximately 18.5km. Historical data are available for years 1980–2009 and were derived from the ERA-Interim dataset. Projections are available for years 2006–2100 and were derived from GFDL-CM3 and NCAR-CCSM4 model outputs using the RCP 8.5 emissions scenario.

A wet day is defined as a day with precipitation accumulation greater than or equal to 1.0mm.

Service endpoints

Min/mean/max summaries (point query)

Query a complete set of wet days per year composed of the ERA-Interim historical baseline (1980–2009) and the entire projected range (2006–2100) for both available models, GFDL-CM3 and NCAR-CCSM4 (both RCP 8.5).

Endpoint Example URL
Historical wet days per year /wet_days_per_year/historical/point/65.0628/-146.1627
Projected wet days per year /wet_days_per_year/projected/point/65.0628/-146.1627
Historical and projected wet days per year /wet_days_per_year/hp/point/65.0628/-146.1627
CSV output is also available by appending ?format=csv to the URL.

Min/mean/max summaries across provided year range (point query)

Query wet days per year for the specified year range. Valid year ranges are between 1980–2009 for historical data and 2006–2100 for projected data.

Endpoint Example URL
Historical wet days per year within year range /wet_days_per_year/historical/point/65.0628/-146.1627/1990/2000
Projected wet days per year within year range /wet_days_per_year/projected/point/65.0628/-146.1627/2040/2060
Historical and projected wet days per year within year range /wet_days_per_year/hp/point/65.0628/-146.1627/1990/2040
CSV output is also available by appending ?format=csv to the URL.

Wet days per year for all years (point query)

Query wet days per year for all models, scenarios, and years (1980–2100):

Endpoint Example URL
Wet days per year for all years /wet_days_per_year/all/point/65.0628/-146.16270
CSV output is also available by appending ?format=csv to the URL.

Output

Min/mean/max summaries

Results from wet days per year min/mean/max queries will look like this:

{
  "historical": {
    "wdpymax": 160,
    "wdpymean": 134,
    "wdpymin": 112
  },
  ...
}

The above output is structured like this:

{
  <era>: {
    "wdpymax": <max number of wet days per year across all models and years used for the summary era time period>,
    "wdpymean": <number of wet days per year across all models and years used for the summary era time period>,
    "wdpymin": <number of wet days per year across all models and years used for the summary era time period>
  },
  ...
}

Wet days per year for all years

Results from wet days per year for all years queries will look like this:

{
  "ERA-Interim": {
    "1980": {
      "wdpy": 124
    },
    "1981": {
      "wdpy": 119
    },
    "1982": {
      "wdpy": 145
    },
    ...
  },
  ...
}

The above output is structured like this:

{
  <model>: {
    <year>: {
      "wdpy": <count of wet days per year>
    },
    ...
  },
  ...
}
